# Gustometer_Stress_BNST_DCM

The repository contains de-identified effective connectivity data and the codes used to support the main findings in the Guerrero-Hreins et al. manuscript.

The present study leverages ultra-high field (UFH) functional MRI and dynamic causal modelling (DCM) to characterise BNST effective connectivity during the processing of food cues and food receipt during high stress, a common trigger of food-related psychopathology. 

Main results
- The Run_PEB_Cue.m script will reproduce the DCM results examining BNST connectivity during high stress food cue presentation, reported in the Guerrero-Hreins et al., manuscript.
- The Run_PEB_MCue.m script will reproduce the DCM results examining BNST connectivity during high stress rewarding food cue presentation, reported in the manuscript.
- The Run_PEB_Taste.m script will reproduce the DCM results examining BNST connectivity during high stress food taste receipt, reported in the manuscript. This script will also reproduce the DCM results exploring the association between BNST effective and connectivity and participant’s self-reported change in stress levels during high stress beverage receipt, reported in the manuscript.
- The Run_PEB_MTaste.m script will reproduce the DCM results examining BNST connectivity during high stress rewarding food taste receipt, reported in the manuscript.
